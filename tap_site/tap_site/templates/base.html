    Brief Introduction

    What is The Accountability Project a.k.a "Wenyenchi"? Wenyenchi is a citizen engagement platform; an online baraza where citizens can engage directly with public and private service providers. It is a meeting point where experts from different parts of the world can assist community members to create homegrown solutions to change their lives for the better. It is also a place where problem-solvers can showcase their solutions, test their prototypes and monitor the socio-economic impact of the solution on the community.

    How does it work? The platform uses a scorecard of between 1 and 5. 1 means that the problem is ongoing and hasn't been solved while 5 means that the problem has been completely solved. In this format citizens, researchers and policy analysts can track the progress of the problem and the success of implemented solutions. Constant Feedback from the community members will help in succesful implementation and evaluation of a solution.

    How will I benefit? The platform provides a scorecard to measure economic progress.The information collected from community members and then analyzed by experts can be used in land use planning, business / entrepreneurship capacity development, natural resource development, utilization of traditional community lands and social capacity. The platform gives opportunities for community members to connect with other community members and problem-solvers from any part of the country or the world and this will improve social cohesion and increase the transfer of knowledge. We will be using the information to create sustainable development models.

    Who is this project for? This project is for everyone; from a high school student, university professor, government official, company executive, journalist, ordinary citizens to university students. There are no qualifications or conditions to be part of the project. We are crowdsourcing problem-solving. We believe that sustainable homegrown solutions are the key to unlocking the mighty potential of the African continent.

    How do I contribute? Register and start reviewing development projects or services. All you need is a smartphone with a camera and an internet connection. Once you have identified a problem in an area. Take a picture and write a review about it. Share it with community members and then the problem-solving starts. Note that the picture is optional. You can write a review without a picture but a picture is worth a 1000 words.

    How does the future look like? We would like to add at least half of the Sub-saharan countries to the platform by 2025 and the rest by 2030. we willbe adding data analysis tools like 3D charts, line graphs, and maps to give users an even richer experience.

    Are you hiring? Our goal is to reach at least 50,000 community members and problem-solvers and then we can start the hiring process, We will be recruiting project managers, animators, writers, data analysts, developers, and other office staff. For now you can volunteer as a mobilizer or a "wenyenchi ambassador". Mobilizers will be considered first whenever a position needs to be filled.

The human mind makes possible all development achievements, from health advances and agricultural innovations to efficient public administration and private sector growth. Only half of women in developing regions receive the recommended amount of health care they need. Water can pose a serious challenge to sustainable development but managed efficiently and equitably, water can play a key enabling role in strengthening the resilience of social, economic and environmental systems in the light of rapid and unpredictable changes.     Problem Solving Approach

    Real World Example Problem - "Unregistered Drones in Kenya" The Kenyan government is having a difficult time trying to ensure that all drones are duly registered.

    Points to Consider Based on security concerns this problem needs to be resolved urgently. Drone technology is being used in many sectors; wildlife tracking to prevent incidences of poaching, search % rescue missions, dropping aid and medicine to far flung areas, 3D mapping and in aerial photography and filmmaking. The government's role is to make sure that Kenyans perform these tasks within the legal requirements of the law.

    Final Solution The final solution is a digital registration platform named "Drone Registry Kenya". This is a web application where drone owners can digitally registered their drones. Each drone owner has an account to add and delete drones which makes it easier for them to update their registration information. The drone owner can also create flight schedules that will be used to monitor unregistered flight schedules and to apprehend non registered drones.

    Uses and limitations of citizen satisfaction data

    “Call to Action” Service delivery data can serve as a “call to action” for governments if the data reveal outcomes that fall short of expectations.(Source: The Life in Transition Survey)

    Diagnostic function Increased data availability can also allow for better measurement of policies and outcomes and for impact evaluation.(Source: The Life in Transition Survey)

    Progress Monitoring Data on peoples’ satisfaction with their experience and perceptions of quality can help complement more objective measures of service delivery (for example, from facilities surveys) to help policy-makers and development practitioners track the progress of country programmes and strategies.(Source: The Life in Transition Survey)

    Presence of Bias There are a number of caveats in the interpretation of satisfaction data. The presence of “externalities” (for example, additional social benefts of education, public health and so on) may mean that user satisfaction does not capture the full extent of desired social outcomes.(Source: The Life in Transition Survey)

    Social Conditioning Even if individuals and society share the same goal – such as improvements in student learning – some people may have been conditioned to have low expectations, leading them to report high satisfaction despite poor service delivery outcomes.(Source: The Life in Transition Survey)

    Service Delivery Standards Reported satisfaction may be disconnected from actual quality of services if people do not have a basis for comparison, or information, about what level of quality and service they should be expecting.(Source: The Life in Transition Survey)
